Why Fee-Based vs. Commission-Based?

Commission-Based Brokers (Typical 8-12% of Sale Price)

  • Conflicts of Interest: Incentivized to push for quick sale
  • Timing Pressure: Motivation to sell now vs. when optimal
  • Lower Valuations Acceptable: Any sale generates commission
  • Limited Value Enhancement: No incentive to improve business first
  • Percentage-Based Costs: Fees scale with transaction size unnecessarily

Fee-Based Advisory (RTO Advisory Model)

  • Aligned Interests: Paid for advice, not sale completion
  • Optimal Timing: Recommend selling only when truly right
  • Value Maximization: Focus on achieving best possible outcome
  • Strategic Preparation: Multi-year value enhancement programs
  • Transparent Costs: Fixed fees known upfront
  • No Pressure: Guidance based solely on your best interests

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I pay upfront fees instead of just a success fee?

Upfront retainers ensure our advice is objective and in your best interests, not influenced by the desire to close a transaction. Success fees alone create pressure to sell quickly rather than optimize timing and value.

How do your fees compare to commission-based brokers?

Commission-based brokers typically charge 8-12% of the sale price. On a $20M transaction, that's $1.6M - $2.4M. Our total fees are typically $100K-400K, a fraction of the cost while delivering superior value through strategic preparation.

What if I decide not to sell after the engagement?

That's perfectly fine. Our goal is to provide objective advice. If the analysis shows selling isn't optimal, we'll tell you and help you build value for a future exit when timing is right.

Can fees be paid over time?

Yes, we structure retainers to be paid in installments aligned with project milestones or monthly for ongoing engagements.

Do you ever work on pure contingency?

No. Pure contingency arrangements create inherent conflicts of interest that undermine our ability to provide objective, client-focused advice.

What size companies do you work with?

We focus on middle-market companies typically with $5M - $100M in revenue or $2M - $20M in EBITDA.
